Environmental Protection

The Irish government prioritizes protecting the natural environment and the health and wellbeing of its citizens. Policies and measures aim to prevent and minimize environmental damage such as air, water, and land pollution. Environmental levies fund initiatives like waste prevention campaigns and environmental education. Civil society, community groups, and NGOs are actively supported in environmental matters.

Waste Policy and the Circular Economy

Ireland is transitioning towards a circular economy focused on reducing waste and maximizing resource use. The Waste Action Plan for a Circular Economy (launched in September 2020) outlines steps to prevent waste generation and improve sustainable management of products and resources, emphasizing waste prevention and efficient resource use.

Funding Supports and Schemes

The government provides various funding sources and supports to encourage citizens, businesses, and organizations to participate in environmental protection and problem-solving initiatives. These supports help develop and implement environmental projects.

EU and International Environmental Policy

Ireland collaborates on environmental protection across borders, including cooperation on the island of Ireland and with neighboring countries. The government contributes to EU and international environmental policies aimed at improving water and air quality, waste management, renewable energy use, and overall environmental sustainability.
