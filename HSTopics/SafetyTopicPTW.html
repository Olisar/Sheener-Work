Permit to Work System

Ensuring Safe Work at Amneal

Our Permit to Work system coordinates safety responsibilities across all operations, contractors, and shifts.

What is Permit to Work?

A formal authorization system designed to ensure safe execution of critical and non-routine activities. It represents the final authorization of a safe system of work, ensuring all Environment, Health & Safety responsibilities are clearly coordinated between contractors, Amneal personnel, and area owners across all shifts. The PTW system identifies hazards, implements controls, and establishes emergency procedures before any high-risk work begins.

When Do You Need a Permit?

All non-routine hazardous work activities require a Permit to Work for both Amneal personnel and contractors. This includes: Confined Space Entry, Hot Work/Open Flame, Work at Height, Line Breaking & Isolations without Energy Control Procedures, Energised Electrical Work, Excavating and Trenching, and Crane Operations. Routine work performed by embedded contractors (canteen, security, cleaning) is excluded unless considered high-risk. Operations by non-embedded contractors require a General Permit to Work.

Key Roles & Responsibilities

Permit User: The work crew supervisor who completes risk assessments, obtains approvals, verifies isolations, and signs off work completion. Amneal Representative: Plans work, determines safe systems, ensures training, and inspects work areas. Area Owner: Coordinates activities in their controlled area and confirms safe execution. EHS Engineer: Audits PTW implementation and approves method statements and risk assessments as required.

The Permit to Work Process

1) Complete mandatory Contractor Induction Training, 2) Conduct worksite assessment and walk-down with Amneal Representative, 3) Develop detailed Method Statement with task-specific Risk Assessments and LOTO plans, 4) Complete permit application with all supporting documentation (training certs, equipment tests), 5) Obtain formal approval from Permit Approver, 6) Conduct pre-job briefing with work team, 7) Execute work strictly per permit conditions, 8) Sign off completion and restore area to clean, safe condition. Permits are valid for one week maximum.

Critical Requirements

Training: All personnel must complete Amneal Induction Training before site access. Documentation: Method Statements and Risk Assessments are mandatory for all Critical Safe Work. Walk-downs: Mandatory pre-work site evaluations with the Amneal Representative. Validity: Permits valid for 7 days maximum; must be closed daily and reopened next day if work continues. Supporting Permits: Separate permits required for Confined Space, Hot Work, Work at Height, and other specific tasks. Archiving: All closed permits must be retained per procedure requirements.
