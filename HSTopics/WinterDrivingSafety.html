Winter Driving Safety Tips

"With winter and colder conditions approaching, staying safe on the roads is more important than ever."

1. Be Alert to the Risk of Fatigue

Fatigue is a year-round issue, but winter driving in darkness and juggling additional seasonal commitments can cause concentration to slip.

2. What to Do When Driving Fatigued

If you feel fatigued, pull over somewhere safe and take a 10-15 minute break, ideally with a coffee if possible.

3. Plan Your Winter Journey in Advance

Winter mornings may mean slower car starts, so plan ahead by getting up at least 10 minutes early to prepare your car. Always clear the windscreen fully before starting your journey.

4. Allow Extra Time for Winter Journeys

Expect delays, especially during morning and night travel. Avoid rushing without fully clearing your windscreen, windows, and mirrors.

5. Be Prepared for Driving in Snow and Ice

Winter can bring snow, high winds, and rain. Drive slower, use gentle maneuvers, and remember that stopping distances are up to 10 times longer in icy conditions.

6. Uphill Driving in Winter Conditions

On snowy or icy roads, use a low gear when driving uphill and leave plenty of distance from the car in front. When driving downhill, reduce speed before reaching the slope, use a low gear, and avoid sudden braking.

7. Wear Proper Footwear for Winter Driving

Wet shoes from rain or snow may slip off pedals. Keep a pair of flat shoes in the car specifically for driving.

8. Watch Out for Breakdowns and Vulnerable Road Users

While pedestrian and cyclist numbers may drop in winter, remain alert and allow extra space when overtaking, especially during high winds when they could be blown off course.

9. What to Do When Encountering a Winter Breakdown

If you encounter a breakdown, slow down, only overtake when it's safe, and use hazard lights to alert others of the obstruction.
