Opening Remarks and Introduction

Scope of the Review

  • The scope of the EHS and Energy Management Review Meeting, as per ISO 14001, and ISO 45001 standards, is to assess and improve the organization’s performance in environmental, health and safety management.
  • The meeting aims to identify areas of improvement, set objectives, and plan actions to enhance the organization’s EHS management systems

Clause 9.3

  • Top Management shall review the organisation’s Health and safety & Environmental, at planned intervals, to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, effectiveness and alignment with the strategic direction of the organisation.
  • Management Review Period: Annually (Mandatory)

MDI Process Context & Key Risks

Ethanol (co-solvent & cleaning) and nitrogen (inerting & purging) used in metered dose inhaler production:

  • External: 1000L IBC storage with flammable mass & vapour cloud risk
  • Internal: Flammable atmosphere from spills, leaks, or vaporisation
  • Nitrogen: Asphyxiation risk in confined/poorly ventilated areas
  • Two supply modes under evaluation: qualified line vs 5L bottles

External Area: Ethanol IBC & Controls

Key Risks: Large inventory, containment loss, vapour cloud, ignition sources

Engineering Controls:

  • Outdoor location with full-volume bunding & impact protection
  • ATEX-rated hoses, anti-static couplings, earthing/bonding
  • Safe nitrogen venting (not into enclosed areas)

Operational: ATEX zoning, PTW for hot work, fire extinguishers (foam), spill response plan

Internal Handling: Two Supply Options

Option A: Supply Line (Preferred)

Reduced manual handling, fewer coupling events, closed-loop transfer, lower vapour exposure

Option B: 5L Bottles (Interim)

Overhead pouring to 2m height, musculoskeletal & splash risk, higher vapour exposure frequency

Evaluate residual risks and implement robust controls for each option

Option A: Supply Line – Controls

Engineering:

  • Double-contained lines, non-return valves, pressure relief
  • High-level alarm & automatic shut-off on leak detection
  • ATEX-compliant equipment, intrinsically safe instruments
  • Adequate ventilation at potential leak points

Procedural:

  • Start-up/shutdown checklists, routine pressure testing
  • Clear emergency isolation & operator training

Option B: 5L Bottles – Interim Controls

Ergonomic & Spill:

  • Use pump/drum trolley; lower vessel inlet to chest height
  • Drip trays, absorbent materials, immediate spill clean-up

Electrical & Exposure:

  • Conductive containers, earth bonding, no synthetic clothing
  • Local extraction/ventilation at vessel top

PPE & Training:

  • Chemical goggles/face shield, ethanol-compatible gloves, flame-retardant coat
  • Manual handling, spill & fire response training

Nitrogen Controls & Regulatory Framework

Nitrogen Controls

  • Vent nitrogen safely outdoors (not into room)
  • O₂ monitoring & alarm where asphyxiation risk exists
  • Confined-space procedures for purged vessels
  • Signage: "Nitrogen – Asphyxiant"

Regulatory (Ireland)

  • SHWW Act 2005: General duty of care
  • Chemical Agents Regulations 2024: Hazard assessment
  • DSEAR: ATEX zoning & risk assessment
  • REACH/CLP: Classification & labelling

Key Recommendations & Action Items

Priority 1: Transition to qualified supply line (Option A)

Priority 2 (Interim): Strengthen 5L bottle controls - ergonomic fixes, PPE audit, training

Immediate Actions:

  • Update ATEX/DSEAR risk assessment & zoning
  • Implement O₂ monitoring plan for nitrogen areas
  • Commission supply line system (medium-term)
  • Ongoing compliance monitoring & training updates
