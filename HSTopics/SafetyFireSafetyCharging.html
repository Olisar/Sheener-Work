Chargers

Safety and Compatibility Tips

"A Quick Guide to Safe Charging Practices

and Risk Prevention."

1. Buy Electrical Chargers from Reputable Retailers

You should always purchase electrical chargers from reputable retailers to ensure safety and compliance with regulations.

2. Check for the CE Mark

Ensure that the charger has the CE mark, which indicates compliance with EU safety standards.

3. Avoid Cheap or Unbranded Chargers

Cheaper chargers, poor-quality ones, or chargers with unclear or misspelled labels may pose risks of electrocution, fire, or device damage. Ensure the manufacturer's contact details and branding are clearly provided.

4. Verify Plug Compatibility

All electrical chargers must be suitable for a standard three-pin wall socket. Avoid chargers with two-pin plugs, as they are not compliant in Ireland.

5. Follow Manufacturer Instructions

The charger should include instructions or a safety manual in clear English or Irish, with details on safe usage, electrical safety guidance, and proper disposal.

6. Tips for Using Chargers Safely

  • Read and follow the manufacturer’s safety manual.

  • Stop using chargers that are difficult to plug in or damaged, and dispose of them safely.

  • Do not leave devices charging overnight or unattended.

  • Avoid charging on flammable surfaces like duvets or pillows.

  • Do not overload wall sockets or daisy-chain extension leads.

7. Beware of Counterfeit Chargers

Even if a charger appears to have a logo of a known brand, it could be counterfeit. Ensure authenticity and compatibility with your device to avoid fire or explosion risks.
