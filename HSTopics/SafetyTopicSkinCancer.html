Skin Cancer Awareness Tips











Protect Your Skin, Protect Your Life

Learn how to spot, prevent, and reduce your risk of skin cancer

1. What is Skin Cancer?

It’s the most common cancer in Ireland, with over 11,000 cases yearly. 9 in 10 cases are caused by UV rays from the sun or sunbeds.

2. Why Early Detection Matters

Spotting skin cancer early increases the chance of a cure to 90%. Know your skin and check monthly for unusual changes.

3. Two Main Types

Non-melanoma cancers like basal and squamous cell types are common, while melanoma is rarer but more serious—especially in younger people.

4. Skin Changes to Watch

Look out for red spots, lumps, scaly patches, ulcers that don’t heal, or changing moles. These can appear anywhere on your body.

5. Symptoms of Melanoma

Melanoma may start as a new mole or a change in an existing one. Irregular shape, color, or size are red flags. Get checked by a doctor.

6. Follow the SunSmart Code

Use sunscreen, cover up, seek shade, and avoid tanning. Reducing UV exposure greatly lowers your cancer risk.

7. Know Your UV Risks

Sun exposure—even on cloudy days—can damage skin cells. The effects build up over time and may lead to skin cancer years later.

8. Monthly Skin Checks

Check your skin head-to-toe every month. Use a mirror or ask someone to help you check hard-to-see areas like your back or scalp.

9. Watch for Changes

Any new spot, or one that changes in shape, size, or color, should be examined by a doctor. Don’t wait—early action saves lives.

10. Share Awareness

Help friends and family understand the risks. The more people know, the more lives we can protect from preventable skin cancer.

11. SPF Isn't Just for Summer

Protect your face with SPF every day, all year round. Even on cloudy or rainy days, UV rays can still damage your skin. Daily sunscreen use helps lower the risk of premature aging and skin cancer.
