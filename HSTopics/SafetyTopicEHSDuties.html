Health and Safety Duties Tips
















The Safety, Health and Welfare at Work Act 2005

duties as an employer and employee under the 2005 Act

1. Conduct Regular Risk Assessments

Identify potential hazards in the workplace and implement measures to mitigate associated risks.

2. Develop and Maintain a Safety Statement

Create a written document outlining your company's health and safety policies, ensuring it's regularly updated and communicated to all staff.

3. Provide Adequate Training and Supervision

Ensure employees receive proper instruction and supervision to perform their tasks safely and competently.

4. Maintain Safe Equipment and Facilities

Regularly inspect and service all machinery and equipment, and ensure workplace facilities meet health and safety standards.

5. Establish Clear Emergency Procedures

Develop and communicate procedures for emergencies, ensuring all employees are familiar with them.

6. Use Provided Protective Equipment

Always wear and correctly use any personal protective equipment supplied by your employer.

7. Report Hazards and Incidents Promptly

Inform your employer immediately about any unsafe conditions or practices observed in the workplace.

8. Adhere to Safety Policies and Procedures

Follow all established safety guidelines and participate in required training sessions.

9. Avoid Improper Conduct

Refrain from engaging in behavior that could endanger yourself or others in the workplace.

10. Maintain Personal Health and Safety

Ensure you are fit for work and not under the influence of substances that could impair your ability to perform tasks safely.
