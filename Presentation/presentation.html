Ethanol – Hazards & Precautions

Classification: H225 - Highly Flammable Liquid and Vapour

Hazards

  • Highly flammable liquid (flash point ~12°C)
  • Vapour explosion risk (flammable range: 3.3%–19% in air)
  • Static ignition and pool fire potential
  • Skin and eye irritation, inhalation hazard

Precautions

  • ATEX-rated equipment with proper earthing and bonding
  • Bunded storage with full-volume containment
  • Local extraction ventilation at transfer points
  • PPE: chemical goggles/face shield, ethanol-compatible gloves, flame-retardant clothing
  • Eliminate ignition sources, implement hot work permit system
  • Spill response plan with appropriate absorbent materials
  • Fire extinguishers (foam-based) readily accessible

Nitrogen (N₂) – Hazards & Precautions

Classification: H280 - Contains Gas Under Pressure | Simple Asphyxiant

Hazards

  • Odourless, colourless asphyxiant gas
  • Rapid expansion: 1L liquid nitrogen → ~700L gas. Displaces oxygen in enclosed or poorly ventilated areas
  • Below 18% O₂: impaired judgment, rapid breathing, dizziness
  • Below 11% O₂: unconsciousness and fatality within minutes
  • Highest risk in confined spaces

Precautions

  • Vent nitrogen safely outdoors only during purging operations (never into enclosed areas)
  • Install O₂ monitoring with alarms (set at 19.5% O₂ minimum)
  • Implement confined-space entry procedures and permits
  • Clear signage: "Nitrogen – Asphyxiant Hazard"
  • Emergency rescue plan with trained personnel
  • Ensure adequate ventilation in all nitrogen-use areas
  • Never enter nitrogen-purged vessels without proper atmospheric testing

Competency Requirements Under Irish Regulations

Legal Definition (SHWW Act 2005, S.8)

"A person shall be regarded as competent where they have sufficient training, experience and knowledge appropriate to the nature of the work to be undertaken."

Handling & Operation Personnel

  • Training: Chemical hazards (Safety Data Sheets), flammable liquid handling procedures, asphyxiant awareness
  • Experience: Supervised operation period before independent work authorization
  • Knowledge: Emergency procedures, PPE selection and use, spill response protocols
  • Certification: ATEX awareness training (for flammable zones), confined-space entry qualification (for N₂ areas)
  • Assessment: Documented competency verification and sign-off before authorization to work independently
  • Refresher: Annual training updates and hazard communication reviews

Regulatory Framework: SHWW Act 2005, Chemical Agents Regulations 2024, DSEAR (ATEX), REACH/CLP

Competency Requirements (Continued)

Maintenance & Inspection Personnel

  • Training: Equipment-specific maintenance procedures, ATEX equipment standards and requirements, pressure systems safety
  • Experience: Qualified technician status (electrical/mechanical trade certification)
  • Knowledge: Leak detection methods, integrity testing procedures, isolation and lock-out/tag-out protocols
  • Certification: Pressure Equipment Directive (PED) training, ATEX maintenance and inspection qualification
  • Refresher: Annual updates on regulatory changes, equipment modifications, and new safety procedures
  • Documentation: Maintenance records, inspection reports, and competency assessment files

Key Compliance Points

  • All personnel must demonstrate competency before being authorized to work with ethanol or nitrogen systems
  • Competency assessments must be documented and retained as evidence of compliance
  • Supervision requirements apply until competency is formally verified and documented
  • Regular competency reviews ensure ongoing compliance with Irish safety regulations

Additional Regulations: Pressure Equipment Directive, Confined Spaces Regulations, Manual Handling Regulations
